title: The Journey Mattered
description: We're all still in the tunnel. Don't tell us there was never any light.

It’s not really about the art. It’s about the part of you that takes materials
and creates a thing that you put out in the world. Just like building a fence.
Or baking a cake from raw-as-practicable ingredients.

This is both grandiose (look what I did!) and self important (look what **I**
did!). And LLM coding agents are radically changing the connection between you &
the materials.

It’s fine that it’s changing.  
Code becoming an unimportant detail is fine.  
Software Practitioners will be fine.  
I will be fine.  

But the refrain of “the code was never the point” in response to the lamentation
of this foundational shift is dismissive & lacks nuance — it lacks respect.

The journey mattered to those **on the fucking journey**. It actually wasn’t
just about the destination, it was how you got to where you were going.

And it **does matter** that people feel a sense of… sadness? Loss? that the
thing they honed, pined for, and got lost in, is going away. What’s being lost —
what’s going away — is not the “typing at the keyboard to put arcane
incantations into the ether”, it’s how it **feels** when you manifest something
useful from nothing and share with an audience — a sense of accomplishment.
